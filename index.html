Fentanyl Help

All members of the Chapman community are encouraged to learn about fentanyl awareness and how to use and administer narcan.

Narcan

/* information written by Charlie and Chloe */

Narcan (naloxone) is available over-the-counter at most pharmacies in Anaheim, including CVS and Walgreens locations. You can also get it for free from the Orange County Health Care Agency's office at 1725 W. 17th Street, Santa Ana. Other local sources include community health centers and harm reduction programs - the LGBT Center OC and APAIT Health Center often provide free Narcan. To administer Narcan nasal spray: remove from the package, insert nozzle into the nostril of the person experiencing overdose while they lay on their back, and press firmly to spray the entire dose. It is recommended to carry two doses of Narcan together at all times. If the first dose was not enough to stop the overdose, you will know within two minutes of administering the Narcan. Watch for withdrawal symptoms after administration. After administering, call 911 immediately and stay with the person. California's Good Samaritan law protects people who assist during overdose emergencies, meaning you won't face arrest for small amounts of drugs when seeking medical help.

Awareness

/* information written by Charlie and Chloe */

Fentanyl is extremely potent, as little as 2 milligrams or about the size of a few grains of salt can be lethal. Many street drugs now contain fentanyl, including counterfeit pills that look exactly like prescription medications. Never use drugs alone, it is best to always have someone present who can respond to emergencies or use virtual spotting services. Be aware that counterfeit pills and other drugs may contain fentanyl without your knowledge. Signs of fentanyl overdose include shallow or no breathing, unresponsiveness, and blue or gray skin tone, as well as pinpoint pupils and gurgling sounds. If you choose to use substances, start with a very small test amount and go slowly. Having an emergency plan and telling someone you trust about your use can help save your life.

Testing

/* information written by Charlie and Chloe */

Fentanyl test strips can be ordered from DanceSafe.org and other harm reduction organizations. In Orange County, they're available from several needle exchange programs and community organizations, contact the OC Needle Exchange Program (OCNEP) for locations and hours. To use test strips: dissolve a small amount of the substance in water following the specific ratios for different substances, dip the strip, and wait 5 minutes for results. However, test strips aren't perfect and they may miss some analogs and won't detect all dangerous substances. The safest approach is to only use substances obtained from legitimate medical sources. If you choose to use, having test strips, never using alone, and keeping Narcan on hand are essential safety practices. Always test each new batch, even from the same source.
